About Tabligh Jamaat

Tabligh Jamaat is a global Islamic missionary movement that focuses on urging Muslims to return to primary Sunni Islam, particularly in matters of ritual, dress, and personal behavior.

History

The Tabligh Jamaat movement was founded in 1926 by Muhammad Ilyas al-Kandhlawi in Mewat, India. The movement began as an effort to reform Muslims who had converted from Hinduism but were still practicing many Hindu customs.

1926

Founded by Maulana Muhammad Ilyas Kandhlawi in Mewat region of India with the aim of encouraging Muslims to practice their religion more diligently.

1944

After the death of Muhammad Ilyas, leadership passed to his son Muhammad Yusuf Kandhlawi who expanded the movement internationally.

1965

The movement established its international headquarters in Nizamuddin, Delhi, India.

Present

Tabligh Jamaat has grown to become one of the largest Islamic movements in the world with followers in over 150 countries.

Principles and Methodology

The Tabligh Jamaat follows six fundamental principles derived from the lives of the companions of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). These principles form the core of their teachings and activities:

Kalimah

The declaration of faith (Shahada) - understanding its meaning and living by it.

Salah

Praying five times a day with congregation (jama'ah) in the mosque whenever possible.

Ilm & Dhikr

Acquiring knowledge of Islam and remembering Allah through daily practices.

Ikram-e-Muslim

Honoring and respecting fellow Muslims with good character and conduct.

Ikhlas-e-Niyyat

Sincerity of intention - reforming one's life in accordance with Islamic teachings.

Dawat-o-Tabligh

Devoting time to go out in the path of Allah to spread His message.

Activities

Tabligh Jamaat organizes various activities to fulfill its mission of encouraging Muslims to practice their faith:

  • Gasht (Visitation): Small groups visit Muslim homes and mosques to invite them to religious gatherings.
  • Ta'lim (Teaching): Daily reading from Islamic texts, particularly Faza'il-e-A'maal.
  • Jamaat (Group): Formation of groups that travel locally or internationally for specified periods (3 days, 40 days, 4 months).
  • Ijtema (Gathering): Large annual gatherings where Muslims from different regions come together.
  • Khuruj (Going Out): The practice of leaving one's home and comforts to spend time in the path of Allah.

Organizational Structure

Tabligh Jamaat operates with a decentralized structure:

  • Markaz (Center): Each country or region has a central mosque that coordinates activities.
  • Shura (Consultation): Decisions are made through consultation among senior members.
  • Amir (Leader): Each jamaat (group) has an appointed leader for the duration of their trip.
  • Mashwara (Discussion): Daily meetings where activities are planned and issues discussed.

The movement avoids formal hierarchy and emphasizes personal responsibility.

Impact and Reach

Tabligh Jamaat has had significant influence on Muslim communities worldwide:

  • Estimated millions of participants worldwide
  • Active in over 150 countries
  • Annual gatherings attract millions of participants (Bishwa Ijtema in Bangladesh is the second largest Muslim gathering after Hajj)
  • Known for its emphasis on personal reformation rather than political activism
  • Has inspired many Muslims to become more practicing
